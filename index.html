    How to Play Nine Men's Morris

    Game Overview

    Nine Men's Morris is a strategic board game where each player tries to form "mills" - three of their pieces in a straight line. When a mill is formed, you can remove one of your opponent's pieces.

    Phase 1: Placement

    Players take turns placing their 9 pieces on vacant intersections on the board. During this phase, if a player forms a mill, they remove one of their opponent's pieces.

    Phase 2: Movement

    After all pieces are placed, players take turns moving one piece along a line to an adjacent vacant point. A piece can only move to connected points. Forming a mill during this phase also allows removing an opponent's piece.

    Special Rules

    • You cannot remove a piece that is part of a mill unless no other pieces are available.
    • A player loses when reduced to 2 pieces or cannot make a legal move.

    Strategy Tips

    • Control the center points when possible
    • Try to set up positions where you can form multiple mills
    • Block your opponent from forming mills
    • Think several moves ahead
